What's ham radio and why does the ionosphere matter?

The amateur radio service in the US is made up of licensed radio operators who participate in a number of different activities surrounding radio use and development. Amateur radio licensees are known colloquially as hams. Some hams like to build radios, some like to compete in contests. While others like to stay in touch with friends around the world.

Our radio is homemade and operates at 14.0574 MHz The ARRL Band Plan. which corresponds to the 20 meter band. We've used it to make contacts with people as far away as Argentina and Sweden from using a smart phone charger brick as a power source and a carbon fiber fishing pole to support a wire antenna.

We can communicate over those distances because our transmitted radio waves bounce off, (refract), the ionosphere. Radios that operate at in the 80 meter tp 6 meter bands, (and even, occasionally, the two meter band), can communicate over the visual horizon using this 'skip' mode.
